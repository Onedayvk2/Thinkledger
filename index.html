The Problem
"A hiring manager in 2026 receives 400 applications for one role. Maybe 5 are real humans who genuinely want the job. They can't tell which 5."

Every filter hiring used to separate signal from noise has been compromised simultaneously. CVs are keyword-matched by AI in minutes. Cover letters are indistinguishable from human writing. LinkedIn profiles are optimised, endorsed by strangers, and quantified with achievements nobody can verify.

The result isn't just inefficiency. It's a crisis of trust. Employers don't trust applications. Candidates don't trust the process. And the people most likely to be filtered out are exactly the ones who think differently — unconventional paths, genuine depth, real experience that doesn't fit a template.

The credential was designed for a world where what you studied and where you worked were hard to fake. That world ended in 2023.
